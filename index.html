FAQ’s

How much does it cost to develop an app?

On average, from $5,000 to $130,000 . The exact price depends on the design complexity, number of features, and specific project requirements. Learn more about factors influencing the costs when contacting us . If you want to know the tailored quote for your project, leave your contacts below.

How long does it take to build an app?

On average, from 2 to 6 months . As with costs, the timeframe depends on the specific project requirements. Fill out the form and share your request with us. We’ll analyze it and provide an estimated cost and timeline.

What do I need to do before starting development?

  1. Fill out the form to share the key information about your project.
  2. Join a kick-off call with our team to get to know each other and elaborate on your request. In the end, we’ll determine if we can help you.
  3. After a kick-off meeting, we’ll provide you with a detailed estimate. It will include expenditures, the project timeline, a team members list, and the tech stack. We’ll walk you through every detail and answer your questions.
  4. If everything meets your requirements, the last step is signing a contract. After that, we’ll get down to your project execution.

Do you sign NDAs?

Yes, we do. If needed, we can sign the NDA before the first meeting.

Can you work with my in-house team?

Yes, we can integrate with your in-house team and collaborate on project tasks. , we explain our approach to working with the client team, including the methodologies we use and examples from previous projects.

How to ensure your code is high-quality?

You can request an external audit of our code. We’ll give access to the code and provide project documentation for your external expert.

Can you hand over the project to my in-house team?

We are always ready to hand-over the project to another team. To do it, we:
  1. Share project documentation to help new developers get up to speed and understand how everything works in the project.
  2. Provide a UI kit to help in-house teams design new features quickly while maintaining design consistency.
  3. Schedule meetings with the new project team to onboard them, provide context, and answer any questions.
  4. Keep in contact via the group chat to address concerns that may arise.
